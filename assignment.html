Significant challenges in xenotransplantation

Rejection: The recipient's immune system attacking the foreign transplant.
Ensuring the functional compatibility of the animal organ within the human body across species barriers.
Minimising the risk of introducing new infectious agents from the animal donor into the human population .
Broader theological, anthropological, psychological, and ethical considerations, as well as legal and procedural aspects

Ethical Issues in Relation to Organ Transplants

Animal Rights: The use of animals in research, including for organ donation, raises ethical questions about animal rights and welfare.
Public Assent: Xenotransplantation introduces the risk of cross-species disease transmission, raising public health concerns
Allocation of Organs: Even if xenotransplantation becomes viable, ethical issues related to organ allocation will persist, considering factors like cost, benefit to different patient
